Week 3 — Ingredient Mastery & Early Dish Design

Navigate Days

Day 1 — Understanding Ingredients

Goal: Recognize the qualities of primary ingredients.

  • Study vegetables: freshness, seasonality, and flavor intensity.
  • Understand proteins: texture, fat content, and cooking behavior.
  • Identify starches: rice, potatoes, pasta — how they behave when cooked.
  • Note ingredients’ natural flavors and ideal pairings.
Exercise: Taste a raw vegetable, then cook it using two different methods. Record flavor, texture, and aroma changes.
Mini Challenge: Select one protein and cook it using two techniques. Compare and document.

Day 2 — Advanced Flavor Profiling

Goal: Analyze ingredients’ flavor components in detail.

  • Smell, taste, and note sweet, salty, acidic, bitter, and umami elements.
  • Experiment with small combinations to see complementary or contrasting effects.
  • Learn to detect dominant vs supporting flavors.
Exercise: Take three vegetables and taste each raw, lightly cooked, and fully cooked. Record all flavor observations.
Mini Challenge: Pair one protein with two vegetables based on complementary flavors.

Day 3 — Cooking Techniques Deep Dive

Goal: Master basic and intermediate cooking methods.

  • Sauté, roast, steam, poach, braise — understand which foods benefit from each.
  • Observe Maillard reactions, caramelization, and moisture retention.
  • Take notes on timing, temperature, and results.
Exercise: Cook one vegetable and one protein using three different methods. Record flavor and texture differences.
Mini Challenge: Combine two methods in a single dish (e.g., seared then roasted).

Day 4 — Early Dish Composition

Goal: Learn how to structure a simple composed dish.

  • Choose a base (starch, grain, or vegetable), main ingredient, and flavor enhancer.
  • Consider textures, flavors, and colors while combining components.
  • Practice mise en place for multiple elements at once.
Exercise: Build a small plate using one protein, one vegetable, and one starch. Focus on balance.
Mini Challenge: Plate it attractively and record observations.

Day 5 — Seasoning & Finishing Techniques

Goal: Apply advanced seasoning and finishing touches.

  • Adjust seasoning at multiple stages: during cooking, midway, and at plating.
  • Use acids, fats, and aromatics to elevate flavors.
  • Learn simple garnishes that enhance visual appeal and taste.
Exercise: Take your Day 4 dish and adjust seasoning and flavor enhancers. Note differences.
Mini Challenge: Add a garnish that adds texture or flavor. Document its impact.

Day 6 — Tasting & Critical Evaluation

Goal: Analyze dishes for balance, texture, and presentation.

  • Compare your dishes to a professional standard (look online or in cookbooks).
  • Evaluate each component: flavor, texture, color, and plating.
  • Reflect on adjustments for improvement.
Exercise: Taste 2–3 dishes you’ve made this week. Write detailed notes in your journal.
Mini Challenge: Adjust one dish based on your notes and taste again.

Day 7 — Weekly Challenge

Goal: Apply all Week 3 lessons in a composed dish.

  • Create a dish with one protein, 2–3 vegetables, and one starch or base.
  • Apply flavor pairing, seasoning, texture contrast, and plating techniques.
  • Document the dish in your journal: workflow, tasting notes, improvements.
Exercise: Build and plate your dish, applying all lessons from Week 3.
Mini Challenge: Photograph your dish and note flavor, texture, and plating details.
