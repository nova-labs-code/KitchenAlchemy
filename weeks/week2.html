Week 2 — Flavor Building & Texture

Navigate Days

Day 1 — Flavor Pairing Fundamentals

Goal: Learn how to pair ingredients for harmony and contrast.

  • Understand complementary flavors (e.g., tomato + basil, lemon + fish).
  • Experiment with contrasting flavors (sweet vs acid, bitter vs fat).
  • Document your observations in a flavor journal.
Exercise: Create three simple pairings using vegetables and seasonings. Taste and record notes.
Mini Challenge: Combine an unexpected pairing and describe the result.

Day 2 — Texture Awareness

Goal: Identify and create varied textures in a dish.

  • Soft, crunchy, creamy, chewy — learn to recognize each.
  • Experiment with cooking methods to change texture.
  • Observe mouthfeel and contrast in small tasting samples.
Exercise: Cook one vegetable three ways to explore texture: steam, roast, sauté.
Mini Challenge: Combine textures in one small dish (e.g., crunchy topping on soft base).

Day 3 — Balancing Flavors

Goal: Adjust seasoning to achieve balanced dishes.

  • Taste for salt, sweetness, acidity, bitterness, umami.
  • Learn to correct a dish using acid, fat, or seasoning.
  • Understand that balance is both sensory and emotional — the dish should feel “complete.”
Exercise: Take one dish from Day 1 or 2 and adjust it to improve balance.
Mini Challenge: Create a “balanced bite” combining protein, vegetable, and seasoning.

Day 4 — Herb & Spice Mastery

Goal: Explore aromatic herbs and spices, and learn proper usage.

  • Experiment with fresh vs dried herbs.
  • Toast spices lightly to release aroma.
  • Pair herbs and spices with main ingredients to highlight flavors.
Exercise: Create 3 small seasoning blends and taste them with plain vegetables.
Mini Challenge: Apply one blend to a dish and note the flavor change.

Day 5 — Layering Components

Goal: Combine multiple elements for depth in a dish.

  • Learn how base, main, sauce, and garnish work together.
  • Experiment with texture and flavor contrasts across components.
  • Document your workflow for reproducibility.
Exercise: Make a small composed plate with 2–3 elements, balancing flavor, texture, and color.
Mini Challenge: Modify one component to see how it affects the whole dish.

Day 6 — Plating Basics

Goal: Begin presenting dishes professionally.

  • Learn the importance of height, color, and focal points.
  • Arrange components thoughtfully to guide the diner’s eye.
  • Practice small portions first to refine plating style.
Exercise: Plate a simple dish from the week with attention to composition and balance.
Mini Challenge: Take a photo, compare to professional plating, and note improvements.

Day 7 — Weekly Challenge

Goal: Apply all skills from Week 2 in one composed dish.

  • Incorporate flavor pairings, textures, seasoning, and plating.
  • Focus on a visually and tastefully balanced dish.
  • Reflect on what worked and what could be improved for Week 3.
Exercise: Create a composed dish combining at least 3 vegetables and 1 protein. Apply all lessons from Week 2.
Mini Challenge: Document your dish and notes in your culinary journal. Evaluate flavor, texture, and presentation.
